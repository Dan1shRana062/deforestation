Causes

Causes

  • Deforestation occurs due to various human activities driven by the need for resources and land. One major cause is agricultural expansion. As the global population grows, there's a higher demand for land to cultivate crops and raise livestock. This often leads to clearing large areas of forests for farming. Logging is another significant contributor to deforestation.
  • Timber is a valuable resource for construction, paper production, and other industries. As a result, extensive logging can lead to the depletion of forests. Urbanization is also a cause, as cities and infrastructure projects require land, prompting the clearing of forests.
  • Additionally, forest fires, whether natural or human-induced, play a role in deforestation, destroying large areas of trees. Finally, the extraction of minerals and oil contributes to deforestation, as it necessitates the clearing of land for mining activities.
